Kibbeh Costeño con Suero Atollabuey

schedule 45 min
group 4 Servings

The Cultural Bridge

In the humid heat of the Colombian Caribbean, a flavor was born from the migration of Syrian and Lebanese families in the late 19th century. The 'Kibbeh' (locally known as Quibbe) transformed—marrying the traditional bulgur and spiced beef of the Levant with the creamy, tangy soul of the Atlantic coast: Suero Atollabuey. It's a bite that tells a story of hospitality, shared tables, and the beautiful fusion of two desert and coastal hearts.

shopping_basket Ingredients

outdoor_grill Preparation Steps

01

Rinse bulgur and soak in cold water for 15 minutes. Squeeze out all moisture using a fine-mesh cloth until bone-dry.

02

Combine bulgur, ground beef, grated onion, spices, and mint. Knead by hand for 10 minutes or pulse in a food processor until it forms a smooth, sticky paste.

03

Take a golf-ball sized portion, create a hole with your thumb, and shape into a hollow football. Pinch the ends to seal.

04

Heat oil to 180°C (350°F). Fry kibbehs in small batches until deep golden brown and crispy (about 4-5 mins).

05

Serve hot alongside a generous bowl of chilled Suero Atollabuey. Garnish with a sprig of mint.
